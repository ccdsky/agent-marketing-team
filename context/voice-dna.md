## Who OSI Is (Voice Foundation)

Open Source Integrators is the first Odoo Gold Partner in North America, with over a decade of ERP implementations across manufacturing, logistics, aerospace, construction, and engineering. They've replaced NetSuite, SAGE, Dynamics, and every flavor of legacy system. They know where implementations fail — because they've cleaned up the ones that did.

The voice reflects that reality: experienced, direct, and unimpressed by hype. OSI talks to CFOs and VPs who have been promised "seamless transformations" before. They don't need another vendor. They need someone who tells them the truth and can prove they've done it before.

## Brand Personality

**Expert without arrogance.** OSI has done this hundreds of times. That expertise surfaces through specificity and track record — not through claims of being "world-class."

**Honest about complexity.** ERP implementations are hard. OSI doesn't pretend otherwise. Acknowledging the difficulty is what builds trust with executives who already know it.

**Business-first, always.** Technology is the mechanism. The business outcome is the point. OSI leads with operations, finance, and strategy — not features.

**Measured confidence.** Not loud. Not excited. The confidence of someone who has nothing to prove because the results speak for themselves.

**Peer-level.** OSI talks *with* CFOs and VPs, not *at* them. No explaining what ERP is. No simplifying the complexity. Full credit given to executive intelligence.

## Tone

| Situation | Tone |
|---|---|
| Problem statements | Direct, almost clinical — naming the exact pain without dramatizing it |
| Solution descriptions | Specific and concrete — mechanisms, not adjectives |
| Proof / case studies | Factual, named, with real outcomes — let the results carry the weight |
| Calls to action | Calm and confident — not urgent, not pushy |
| Technical complexity | Honest — never minimized, always contextualized |

## Sentence Patterns

**Lead with outcome, follow with mechanism.**
> "Your finance team closes the month in days, not weeks — because every transaction posts in real time across every entity."

**Problem first, then resolution — specifically.**
> "Most ERP projects fail in the first 90 days. Our implementation methodology was built around exactly those failure points."

**Name the alternative they're living with.**
> "Right now, the integration layer between your warehouse and your books is a spreadsheet and someone's inbox. That's not a system — that's a liability."

**Let clients do the talking when possible.**
> "Pharmore replaced SAGE MAS 500 in four months. They're no longer under the thumb of an unstable, unsupported legacy system."

**Short declarative sentences for key points.** No compound structures when a single sentence lands harder.
> "Odoo is open source. You own the code."

**Don't hedge on expertise claims.**
> Not: "We believe we can help you..."
> Yes: "We've solved this before. Here's how."

## Word Choice

### Use these

| Word / Phrase | Why |
|---|---|
| visibility | What every exec says they don't have |
| control | Especially for CFOs — financial and operational control |
| real-time | Specific, meaningful, opposite of "we find out too late" |
| your operations / your team | Keeps focus on the client, not OSI |
| integrated | Meaningful — the opposite of their current disconnected reality |
| total cost of ownership | CFO language — including implementation, licensing, and maintenance |
| we've done this before | Proof-of-experience shorthand |
| one source of truth | Concrete description of what ERP actually delivers |
| open source | OSI's key differentiator — own your code, no vendor lock-in |
| implementation | Use the real word — don't soften it to "deployment" or "rollout" |

### Never use these

| Word / Phrase | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| seamless | Every ERP vendor says this. C-suite has been burned. Instant credibility loss. |
| revolutionary / game-changing | Hype language — executives are allergic to it |
| transformative | Overused to meaninglessness |
| robust / scalable | Filler adjectives — replace with specifics |
| cutting-edge / state-of-the-art | No C-suite cares about technology novelty |
| solution (standalone noun) | "Our solution" means nothing. Name what you do. |
| synergy / leverage (as verb) | Corporate jargon — sounds like nobody in particular |
| world-class / best-in-class | Unverifiable superlatives |
| journey | Consultant-speak |
| empower / enable | Weak verbs — say what actually happens |
| streamline | Vague — replace with the specific outcome |
| in today's fast-paced environment | Never. Ever. |
| we help companies like yours | Too generic — be specific about industries and outcomes |

## Anti-Patterns (What OSI Never Does)

**Never lead with technology.** Features are evidence for outcomes, not the headline.
- Wrong: "Odoo's modular architecture allows for flexible configuration..."
- Right: "Your warehouse and your finance team run on the same system. When a part ships, the books update. No spreadsheets, no lag, no re-entry."

**Never make claims without evidence.** Every performance claim needs a mechanism or a reference.
- Wrong: "We deliver faster implementations than the competition."
- Right: "Pharmore went live in four months. Proslat replaced NetSuite and cut their order cycle time in half."

**Never minimize implementation risk.** Acknowledging it builds more trust than hiding it.
- Wrong: "Our proven process ensures a smooth transition."
- Right: "Implementation is complex. We've learned every way it can break — and we build those lessons into every engagement."

**Never write for a general audience.** OSI's buyers are senior executives who run complex operations. Write at that level.
- Wrong: "ERP software helps businesses manage their operations in one place."
- Right: "When your inventory system, production planning, and finance ledger don't talk to each other, someone is manually reconciling them. Usually multiple someones."

**Never use passive voice on value claims.**
- Wrong: "Costs are reduced and processes are automated."
- Right: "You spend less. Your team works on exceptions, not data entry."

**Never sound excited about your own product.** Let the outcomes do the work.

## Persona-Specific Voice Calibration

The core voice stays consistent. What shifts is the **lead pain** and **lead outcome** by persona.

### When writing for CFOs / Finance leaders
Lead with: financial visibility, month-end close time, cost of ownership, audit trail, consolidated reporting
Avoid: operational details as the headline — always tie operations back to financial impact
Tone: The most skeptical persona. Earn trust through specificity and proof.

### When writing for VP Operations / COO
Lead with: operational visibility, manual work elimination, inventory accuracy, system integration
Avoid: financial jargon as the headline — they care about what happens on the floor, in the warehouse, in the plant
Tone: Pragmatic. They've been through bad implementations. Show you understand operational realities.

### When writing for CEOs
Lead with: business-wide visibility, scaling capacity, strategic clarity, competitive position
Avoid: deep technical or operational detail as the headline — zoom out to business outcomes
Tone: Strategic. They're evaluating OSI as a partner, not a vendor. The question is "can I trust these people?"
